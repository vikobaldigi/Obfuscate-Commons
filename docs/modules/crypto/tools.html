Open-Source Crypto Tools (TOOLS)

Encryption tools built for users — not clouds, not platforms. Free to inspect, modify, and trust on your own terms.

Why Tools Matter

Encryption is only as strong as the tool you use. Whether you're messaging, storing files, or sending email — the tool shapes your risk, your power, and your privacy.

These tools aren’t built for surveillance capitalism. They’re for autonomy, resistance, and everyday defense.

Email & Identity

  • Thunderbird + Enigmail: Encrypted email via OpenPGP.
  • Tutanota / ProtonMail: Privacy-first email services with end-to-end encryption.
  • GPG: The classic tool for signing and encrypting data and communications.

File Encryption & Backup

  • VeraCrypt: Disk encryption tool — encrypts partitions or entire drives.
  • Cryptomator: Encrypt files before syncing them to the cloud.
  • age: Simpler alternative to GPG. Lightweight, modern, and easy to use.

Messaging & Communication

  • Signal: End-to-end encrypted messages and calls. Open-source. Metadata-minimized.
  • Briar: Peer-to-peer encrypted messaging — no central server, works over Bluetooth or Wi-Fi.
  • Session: Anonymous routing over the Loki network — no phone number needed.

Command Line & Scripting

  • gpg: Encrypt, decrypt, and sign files using OpenPGP.
  • age: Minimal CLI encryption tool using modern crypto (X25519, ChaCha20-Poly1305).
  • openssl: Swiss-army knife of crypto — supports TLS, ciphers, certificates, hashes.

Resisting Surveillance

  • Tor Browser: Anonymous browsing via onion routing.
  • Librewolf: Hardened Firefox fork with telemetry removed.
  • GrapheneOS: Secure Android OS with privacy-focused defaults and verified boot.

Browsers & Search

  • Brave: Chromium-based browser with tracker blocking, HTTPS upgrades, and optional Tor tabs.
  • DuckDuckGo: Search engine that doesn’t log queries or track users.
