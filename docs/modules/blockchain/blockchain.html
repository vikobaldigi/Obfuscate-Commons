What Is a Blockchain?

A blockchain is a distributed database where each record is linked to the one before it using cryptographic hashes. These records, or "blocks," form an immutable chain of verified transactions that can't be altered without detection.

Instead of a central authority validating actions, a blockchain relies on cryptographic consensus ‚Äî rules enforced by math and public agreement.

Where Encryption Comes In

Blockchains use a mix of cryptographic techniques:

  • Hashing (SHA-256): Ensures the integrity of each block
  • Public-Key Cryptography: Users sign transactions with their private key
  • Merkle Trees: Efficiently summarize large sets of transactions
These allow for secure authentication, non-repudiation, and verifiable history ‚Äî without a central gatekeeper.
