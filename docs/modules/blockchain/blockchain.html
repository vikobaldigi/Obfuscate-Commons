What Is a Blockchain?

A blockchain is a shared digital ledger. It records transactions in a list of blocks, where each block is locked to the one before it using a cryptographic hash.

Beyond Money

Cryptocurrency is just the beginning. Blockchains also support:

  • Smart Contracts: Code that runs automatically when conditions are met.
  • DAOs: Groups that run on-chain without central leaders.
  • Digital Identity: Verified credentials and access systems that live on-chain.

Problems and Politics

Blockchain is often praised for decentralization. But in practice, a few actors often hold power ‚Äî through tokens, mining rigs, or code control.

It also uses a lot of energy, can be hard to govern, and sometimes becomes a tool for speculation instead of social benefit.

Current Events: Crypto in 401(k)s

In May 2025, the Trump administration took steps to ease restrictions on including private cryptocurrency assets in retirement plans like 401(k)s. According to reporting by Bloomberg, CNBC, and the Financial Times, this deregulatory move was framed as empowering individual investors.

Critics argue the decision prioritizes financial speculation over long-term security. It may expose retirement funds to volatile and poorly regulated markets under the guise of choice and innovation. Rather than democratizing finance, this shift risks entrenching risk at the expense of vulnerable savers ‚Äî all while shielding institutional actors from accountability.
